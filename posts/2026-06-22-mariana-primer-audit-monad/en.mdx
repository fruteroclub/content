Mariana Ríos showed up with a half-finished remittances bot and an uncomfortable
question: how do I know my contract does what I say it does? That question led to
her first complete **security audit**, done in the open.

Over two weeks she reviewed the contract line by line, documented every
assumption, and published her findings publicly. It was not a theoretical
exercise: she found a reentrancy bug that would have drained the liquidity pool
on the very first real transaction.

What set her work apart was not the tooling but the method: review in public,
leave a verifiable record of every decision, and invite peer review. That is the
standard we want to make normal in the community.
